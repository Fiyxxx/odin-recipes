Xiao Long Bao

Description

Xiao Long Bao are delicate, handmade dumplings filled with juicy, seasoned pork and a rich, gelatinized broth that melts into a savory soup when steamed. Originating from Jiangnan cuisine, these dumplings are skillfully pleated to encase the filling and are traditionally served with black vinegar and julienned ginger. The combination of soft, thin dough, flavorful meat, and hot, soupy broth creates a luxurious and satisfying bite, making them a beloved specialty in dim sum restaurants worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 300g ground pork
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 cups pork or chicken stock
  • 10g gelatin powder (or 4 gelatin sheets)

Steps

  1. Heat the stock and dissolve the gelatin. Pour into a shallow dish and refrigerate until set. Cut into small cubes.
  2. Mix flour, salt, and warm water. Knead into a smooth dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Combine all filling ingredients. Mix well and fold in the soup gelatin cubes.
  4. Roll out the dough into small 8cm circles. Place 1 tbsp of filling in the center, pleat, and seal the dumplings.
  5. Line a bamboo steamer with parchment paper or cabbage leaves. Steam over boiling water for 8-10 minutes.

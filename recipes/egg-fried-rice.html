Egg Fried Rice

Description

Egg Fried Rice is a simple yet flavorful dish made by stir-frying day-old rice with scrambled eggs, garlic, and vegetables, then seasoning it with soy sauce, white pepper, and sesame oil. The high heat of the wok creates a light, fluffy texture, while the eggs coat each grain of rice, adding richness and depth of flavor. Quick to prepare and endlessly customizable, this dish is a staple in Chinese households, often enjoyed as a standalone meal or as a complement to other dishes.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked day-old jasmine rice
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil

Steps

  1. Prepare the eggs: Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok over medium heat. Pour in the beaten eggs and scramble lightly. Once set, break into pieces and remove from the pan.
  2. Stir-fry aromatics: Add the remaining oil and saut√© garlic until fragrant.
  3. Cook the rice: Add rice and break up clumps with a spatula. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Mix in vegetables & eggs: Add mixed vegetables and cooked eggs, stir-frying for another 2 minutes.
  5. Season: Pour in soy sauce, salt, and white pepper. Stir well. Drizzle sesame oil and toss in scallions before serving.

Ingredients

  • 300g silken or medium-firm tofu, cut into 2cm cubes
  • 150g minced pork (or beef)
  • 1 tbsp doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp Sichuan peppercorns, toasted and ground
  • 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (slurry)
  1. Prepare the tofu: Bring a pot of water to a gentle boil and add a pinch of salt. Gently simmer the tofu cubes for 2 minutes to firm them up, then drain and set aside.
  2. Toast Sichuan peppercorns: In a dry pan, toast Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Grind into a fine powder and set aside.
  3. Cook the meat: Heat vegetable oil in a wok over medium heat. Add minced pork and stir-fry until browned.
  4. Aromatics & seasoning: Add minced garlic, ginger, doubanjiang, and douchi. Stir-fry until fragrant. Pour in Shaoxing wine and soy sauce.
  5. Simmer the tofu: Add chicken broth, sugar, and chili flakes. Gently add the tofu cubes and simmer for 3-5 minutes.
  6. Thicken the sauce: Stir in the cornstarch slurry and cook until the sauce thickens.
  7. Finish & serve: Sprinkle ground Sichuan peppercorns and chopped scallions over the dish. Serve hot with steamed rice.
